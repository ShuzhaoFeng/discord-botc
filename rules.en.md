# Blood on the Clocktower: Trouble Brewing — Rules

## Overview

_Blood on the Clocktower: Trouble Brewing_ is a social deduction game for 5–15 players and the **beginner script** of Blood on the Clocktower. Players are divided into the Good team and the Evil team, working toward their respective win conditions through daytime discussion and nighttime actions. The game is hosted by a **Storyteller**, who does not compete for either side and is responsible for assigning roles, running the game flow, and resolving all night actions.

---

## Teams & Win Conditions

| Team     | Roles                | Win Condition              |
| -------- | -------------------- | -------------------------- |
| **Good** | Townsfolk, Outsiders | Execute the Demon          |
| **Evil** | Minions, Demon       | Reduce living players to 2 |

**Special win/loss conditions:**

- If the **Saint** is executed, the Good team **immediately loses**
- If the **Mayor** is alive and only 3 players remain with no execution that day, the Good team **immediately wins**

## Game Flow

The game alternates between **Night** and **Day** phases until one team achieves its win condition.

### Night Phase

All players close their eyes. The Storyteller wakes each role in a fixed order to perform their ability in secret.

**Night 1** (information gathering; the Imp does not kill):

1. Poisoner chooses a player to poison
2. Washerwoman receives their information
3. Librarian receives their information
4. Investigator receives their information
5. Chef receives their information
6. Empath receives their information
7. Fortune Teller receives their information
8. Spy views the Grimoire
9. Butler chooses their master

**Night 2 onwards (each night):**

1. Poisoner chooses a poison target
2. Monk chooses a protection target
3. Spy views the Grimoire
4. Scarlet Woman (activates if triggered)
5. Imp chooses a kill target
6. Ravenkeeper (activates if killed tonight)
7. Empath receives their information
8. Fortune Teller receives their information
9. Undertaker receives their information
10. Butler chooses their master

### Day Phase

All players open their eyes and discuss freely. After discussion, the **nomination and execution** process begins:

- Any player may nominate another player
- Each player may **nominate only once** per day
- Each player may **be nominated only once** per day
- After the nominee defends themselves, all players vote
- A nomination requires votes from **more than half** of the current living players to succeed
- The player with the most valid votes that day is executed; ties result in no execution
- Players may also choose **not to execute** anyone that day

---

## Death & Ghost Rules

- Dead players **may still speak** and participate in discussion
- Dead players lose their role ability
- Dead players retain **one ghost vote** that they may use on any future day; once used, it is gone

---

## Core Mechanics

### Drunk & Poisoned

When a player is **drunk** or **poisoned**:

- Their role ability **does not function**
- The Storyteller will still provide information, but it **may be false**
- The affected player **is not told** they are in this state

### Information Interference

Certain roles cause misleading information:

- The **Spy** (Evil) may register as Good to detection abilities
- The **Recluse** (Good) may register as Evil or even as the Demon to detection abilities

---

## Role Reference

### Demon

#### Imp

Each night (except the first), choose a player to **kill**.

**Special ability:** If the Imp chooses to **kill themselves** at night, they become a ghost and one **Minion secretly becomes the new Imp**, continuing the game.

---

### Minions

#### Poisoner

Each night, choose a player. That player is **poisoned** until dusk the following day. While poisoned, their ability malfunctions and the Storyteller gives them false information.

#### Spy

Each night, may view the **Grimoire**, learning the true identity and status of all players. The Spy may **register as Good** (Townsfolk or Outsider), even if dead, to detection abilities, at the Storyteller's discretion.

#### Scarlet Woman

If the Demon (Imp) dies while **5 or more players** are alive, the Scarlet Woman **immediately becomes the new Imp** and the game continues. (Travellers do not count for this threshold.)

#### Baron

At the start of the game, **2 extra Outsiders** are added to play (and 2 Townsfolk are removed accordingly). The Baron has no night action during the game.

---

### Townsfolk

#### Washerwoman

On the first night, learns that **one of two players** is a particular **Townsfolk** character. The other player may be any role.

#### Librarian

On the first night, learns that **one of two players** is a particular **Outsider** character. If no Outsiders are in play, learns "0 Outsiders are in play" instead.

#### Investigator

On the first night, learns that **one of two players** is a particular **Minion** character.

#### Chef

On the first night, learns **how many pairs of Evil players are sitting adjacent to each other** (i.e., the number of Evil player pairs who are direct neighbors in the seating order).

#### Empath

Each night, learns **how many of their two living neighbors** (0, 1, or 2) belong to the **Evil team**.

#### Fortune Teller

Each night, chooses two players and learns whether **either one is the Demon** (yes/no).
**Note:** At the start of the game, the Storyteller designates one innocent good player as the **Red Herring**. The Fortune Teller receives a "yes" answer whenever the Red Herring is one of the chosen players.

#### Undertaker

Each night (except the first), if a player was executed that day, learns the **true role** of the executed player.

#### Monk

Each night (except the first), chooses a player (not themselves). That player **cannot be killed by the Demon tonight**.

#### Ravenkeeper

If killed **at night**, immediately wakes up, chooses a player, and learns that player's **true role**.

#### Virgin

The first time the Virgin is nominated, if the nominator is a **Townsfolk**, the nominator is **immediately executed** without a vote.

#### Slayer

**Once per game**, publicly declares and chooses a player during the day. If that player is the **Demon**, the Demon **dies immediately**. If the choice is wrong, nothing happens.

#### Soldier

The Demon **cannot kill the Soldier** at night. (The Soldier can still be poisoned by the Poisoner.)

#### Mayor

If exactly **3 players** are alive and **no execution** occurs that day, the Good team **immediately wins** (provided the Mayor is alive).
Additionally, if the Demon chooses the Mayor as a kill target at night, the Storyteller **may redirect the kill** to another player.

---

### Outsiders

Outsiders belong to the Good team, but their presence typically **works against** the Good team.

#### Butler

Each night (starting night 1), must secretly choose a player (not themselves) as their **master**. The following day, the Butler **may only vote if their master is also voting**.

#### Drunk

The Drunk **does not know they are the Drunk**. The Storyteller tells the Drunk they are a particular Townsfolk character, but their "ability" **never works** and the information they receive **may be false** (the Storyteller is encouraged to give false information, but it may coincidentally be true).

#### Recluse

The Recluse may **register as Evil** to detection abilities, and may even register as a specific Minion or Demon character, even if dead, despite being Good. The Storyteller decides when this effect applies.

#### Saint

If the Saint is **executed**, the Good team **immediately loses**.

---

## Strategy Tips

### Good Team

- Cross-reference the first-night information roles (Washerwoman, Librarian, Investigator, Chef) to build a consistent picture
- The Empath and Fortune Teller provide ongoing nightly information — keep them alive
- Account for the Poisoner, Spy, Recluse, and Drunk when evaluating suspicious or contradictory information
- Be cautious about executing the Saint — an accidental execution is an immediate loss
- When only 3 players remain, consider whether the Mayor win condition is achievable

### Evil Team

- The Imp should claim a believable Townsfolk identity and blend into the Good team's trust network
- The Poisoner should prioritize high-value information roles (Fortune Teller, Empath, Undertaker)
- Use the Spy's information carefully to avoid drawing suspicion
- The Imp can choose to self-kill and pass the role to a Minion as a last resort
- The Scarlet Woman should stay low-profile and survive — she is the failsafe if the Imp is executed

---

## Storyteller Responsibilities

The Storyteller is the game's host and referee. Their responsibilities include:

- Secretly assigning all roles before the game begins (using the Grimoire)
- Waking each role in order during the night and providing information
- Ensuring all information is **technically consistent with the rules** (including deliberately providing false information to drunk or poisoned players)
- Tracking death, poison, and protection states
- Running the daytime discussion, nomination, and voting process
- Announcing executions and deaths
- Revealing all role identities at the end of the game
